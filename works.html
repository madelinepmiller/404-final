The Hunger Games Series

The Hunger Games

In a dystopian future, a teenage girl named Katniss Everdeen volunteers to compete in the annual Hunger Games, a televised fight to the death between teenagers from different districts. Katniss fights to survive the brutal Games and defy the Capitol's oppressive regime. As the Games unfold, Katniss becomes a symbol of hope for the citizens of Panem and sparks a rebellion that will change the course of history.

Catching Fire

"Catching Fire" is the second book in Suzanne Collins' "The Hunger Games" trilogy. It picks up where the first book left off, with Katniss and Peeta returning home as victors of the Hunger Games, but struggling to cope with the aftermath of the brutal competition. As rebellion begins to simmer in the districts, Katniss becomes a symbol of hope and defiance, leading her to become a target of the Capitol's wrath once again as she and Peeta are forced back into the arena for the 75th Hunger Games.

Mockingjay

"Mockingjay" is the third and final book in Suzanne Collins' "The Hunger Games" trilogy. After surviving two Hunger Games and becoming a symbol of hope for the districts, Katniss becomes the face of the rebellion against the Capitol. As the war intensifies and the stakes become higher, Katniss must confront her own inner demons and make difficult choices that will determine the fate of Panem.

Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes

"The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes" is a prequel to Suzanne Collins' "The Hunger Games" trilogy, set 64 years before the events of the first book. The story follows a young Coriolanus Snow, who is a mentor in the 10th Hunger Games and determined to rise to power in the Capitol. As he forms a bond with his tribute, Lucy Gray Baird, Snow navigates the politics of the Games and the Capitol, ultimately setting the stage for the events of the original trilogy.

The Gregor Series

Gregor the Overlander

"Gregor the Overlander" is a children's fantasy novel by Suzanne Collins. The story follows Gregor, an 11-year-old boy who falls through a grate in his laundry room and finds himself in the underground world of the Underland, where he discovers a world of giant creatures and a prophecy that involves him and his younger sister. As Gregor goes on a quest to fulfill the prophecy and find his sister, he learns important lessons about bravery, loyalty, and sacrifice.

Gregor and the Prophecy of Bane

"Gregor and the Prophecy of Bane" is the second book in Suzanne Collins' "Underland Chronicles" series. Gregor returns to the Underland, where he discovers a new prophecy involving a deadly rat named Bane who threatens to destroy the fragile peace between the humans and the rats. As he and his friends embark on a dangerous journey to stop Bane, Gregor faces difficult choices and learns more about the mysterious world beneath his feet.

Childrens Books

Year of the Jungle

"Year of the Jungle" is a children's book by Suzanne Collins that tells the story of a young girl named Suzy coping with her father's deployment to Vietnam. The book follows Suzy's journey as she navigates the challenges of having a parent at war, from missing her father to coping with the fear and uncertainty of living in a country torn apart by conflict.

"The author's spot-on memories paired with child-friendly art create a universal exploration of war and its effect on young children, ideally shared with and facilitated by a sensitive adult." - School Library Journal
